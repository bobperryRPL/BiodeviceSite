Fabrication

Lithography

Lithography is a core microfabrication process used to define patterns and features at the microscale. It is commonly used to create masters for microfluidics, define device geometries, and build multilayer process flows.

Not sure whether your project needs lithography? Tell us what you’re building and we’ll help route you.

Contact for guidance Back to What We Offer Access & Training

What this enables

  • Photomask-based patterning for microstructures
  • Masters for PDMS molding and microfluidic channel definition
  • Structured layers for device fabrication workflows
  • Repeatable feature definition for prototyping and small runs

Typical inputs / outputs

  • Inputs: substrate (e.g., wafer), mask file/mask, process requirements (feature size, layers)
  • Outputs: patterned resist, etched/deposited structure (depending on downstream steps)

Requirements

Access depends on training, safety, and tool readiness.

  • Complete required training
  • Follow cleanroom and chemical handling protocols
  • Book time (if self-serve) or request staff support
