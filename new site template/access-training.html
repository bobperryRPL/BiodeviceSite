New users: the typical onboarding path

Access to the BioDevice Foundry is managed through a structured user program designed to ensure safety, efficiency, and project success.

  1. Create a user account (or request access if you don’t have one yet)
  2. Complete required training based on your intended tools and processes
  3. Book equipment or request services depending on your workflow

Training

Training requirements depend on the tools and process categories you plan to use (e.g., cleanroom processes, wet chemistry, machining, or characterization). Some tools may be staff-operated; others may become self-serve after training.

Typical training components

  • Facility orientation (rules, expectations, and best practices)
  • Safety and hazard-specific training (as applicable)
  • Tool-specific training (hands-on)
  • Competency sign-off before independent use (when self-serve is permitted)

Booking and using equipment

Once you have the required training and permissions, you can book equipment (when available for self-serve) through NEMO. For staff-assisted work or specialized workflows, contact us to coordinate.

Two common pathways

  • Self-serve: trained users book time and operate approved tools independently
  • Staff-assisted: staff perform or support critical steps, complex tools, or specialized processes

Before you book

  • Confirm training status
  • Check tool readiness and scheduling constraints
  • Ensure your materials are compatible and approved
