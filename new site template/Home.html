What is the BioDevice Foundry?

The BioDevice Foundry is a multidisciplinary facility that helps turn biomedical ideas into real, working devices.

We support projects that require precision fabrication, rapid prototyping, surface chemistry, and device characterization — with access to specialized equipment and expert staff.

Whether you are developing a microfluidic chip, biosensor, implantable component, or custom research tool, the Foundry provides the infrastructure and support to move your work forward.

Who uses the Foundry?

Our facility supports a wide range of users, including:

  • UBC undergraduate and graduate students
  • Academic researchers and research groups
  • Industry collaborators and startups
  • External academic partners

New users are welcome. Training and onboarding are provided based on your project needs and experience level.

Why people come here

Many users arrive with a concept, a need for specialized fabrication, or a device that must integrate materials, mechanics, and biology. The Foundry is built to support that full journey — from prototype to tested system.

What can you do here?

Users of the BioDevice Foundry can:

  • Fabricate micro- and nano-scale structures
  • Rapidly prototype custom mechanical and polymer parts
  • Functionalize surfaces for biological applications
  • Assemble and package devices
  • Characterize materials and finished devices
  • Receive design and process support from experienced staff

Good to know

You don’t need to arrive with a perfect process plan. If you can describe your goal and constraints, we can help identify the right pathway — whether that’s self-serve equipment access, staff-assisted services, or a hybrid approach.

How do I get started?

Access to the BioDevice Foundry is managed through a structured user program designed to ensure safety, efficiency, and project success.

The typical process includes:

  1. Creating a user account
  2. Completing required training
  3. Booking equipment or requesting services
